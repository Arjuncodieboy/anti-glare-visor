The problem has changed

Headlights are brighter, higher mounted, and harsher than ever. What once improved visibility now often overwhelms it.

Glare isn’t just uncomfortable — it temporarily degrades contrast, depth perception, and reaction time. For many, it turns night driving into a constant compromise.

It’s not just brightness

The issue isn’t simply “too much light”. It’s how modern light interacts with the human eye — scattering, contrast loss, and sudden intensity changes.

Darkening everything helps glare, but destroys visibility. The real solution lies in optics, not tint.

The idea

A lightweight, attachable optical element that selectively reduces headlight glare while preserving the rest of the visual scene.

No electronics. No batteries. No permanent changes. Designed to work with what you already use.

Current status

This is an early product concept approaching completion. The focus is on getting the optics, form, and experience right before release.
